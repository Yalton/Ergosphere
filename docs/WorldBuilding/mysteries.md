## Core Setting Framework

**Year**: 2157
**Humanity's State**: 
- Reached interstellar travel but only within 50 light-years of Earth
- Multiple competing corporations/governments control different sectors
- Communication with Earth takes months/years depending on distance
- Most stations operate semi-autonomously

**The Station**:
- Built by the Thoth Corporation (connecting to your THOTH-OS terminals)
- Official purpose: "Deep space anomaly research and observation"
- Located at the edge of known space, 47 light-years from Earth
- Crew rotation happens every 2 years (you're 18 months in)

## The Big Mysteries

### The Black Hole
- **What we're told**: It's a "stable micro-singularity" perfect for studying gravitational phenomena
- **What's strange**: 
  - It doesn't behave like other black holes
  - Sometimes appears to "respond" to observation
  - The telescope alignment isn't actually for viewing - it's for something else
  - Why does looking at it too long cause headaches/nausea?

### The Station's True Purpose

- Why is a food production facility needed for a 6-person crew?
- Why are there areas of the station you've never been allowed to access?
- The emergency protocols seem designed for threats that don't make sense
- Why does the station need enough power to run a small city?

### The Disappearances

- Previous crew rotations had "incidents" 
- Records are mysteriously corrupted
- Personal items from previous crews still turn up in strange places
- Why are there exactly 6 crew members, always 6, never more or less?

### Time Anomalies

- Clocks occasionally show different times in different parts of the station
- Personal logs sometimes have dates that haven't happened yet
- Dreams about events that later come true
- Why do some tasks feel like you've done them hundreds of times before?

### The Corporation

- Thoth Corp's logo is an eye (Egyptian god of wisdom/moon/JUDGMENT)
- All communication with Earth goes through Thoth filters
- Your employment contract has sections you can't remember signing
- Why do they need "biographical data" collected daily?

### The Emergency Protocols

- "Oxygen failure" - but the station has triple redundancy
- "Power outages" - despite the reactor being overbuilt
- "Heatsink failures" - in the cold of space?
- What are these systems REALLY failing to contain?

### Personal Mysteries

- Why can't you remember applying for this job?
- Your dreams are getting stranger - are they dreams?
- Sometimes you hear your crewmates in places they couldn't be
- Why does the food all taste like memories of Earth?

### The Data

- What is the "biographical data" actually measuring?
- Why must it be transmitted even during emergencies?
- The download terminals process terabytes - of what?
- Why does the data corruption always form patterns?

## Environmental Storytelling Opportunities

- Scratch marks inside maintenance shafts
- Personal logs that cut off mid-sentence
- Areas with too many emergency supplies
- Rooms with furniture arranged to block something
- Burn marks in places that shouldn't have seen fire
- Emergency protocols for scenarios that seem impossible
- Warning signs for hazards that don't exist (or do they?)

## The Beauty of No Answers

The key is that each mystery should have multiple plausible explanations:
- Scientific experiment gone wrong
- Corporate conspiracy  
- Alien influence
- Time loop/paradox
- Psychological breakdown
- Dimensional anomaly
- Or multiple things being true at once

This lets players construct their own horror - their imagination fills the gaps with whatever scares them most.
