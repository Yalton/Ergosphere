# Thoth Observatory

> You're the steward of a failing research station studying humanity's key to FTL communication. Keep the lights on. Gather the data. Try not to lose your mind.

Thoth Observatory is a short-form atmospheric horror game where you play as the sole crew member of a deep space research station orbiting the Sejanus Singularity—a black hole that may hold the secret to faster-than-light communication.

You weren't hired to be a hero. You were hired because you were cheap. The people who built this station couldn't afford more than one crew member at a time, so you're the research scientist, the mechanic, and the janitor all rolled into one.

![HERMES Terminal](/images/logs.png)

## Gameplay

Each day, the station's machine intelligence—HERMES—assigns you a list of tasks. These range from routine maintenance to full-blown emergencies:

- Interact with station terminals to follow procedures or solve puzzles.
- Use portable tools to scan for anomalies throughout the station.
- Take emergency action when systems fail or when the singularity leaks harmful radiation inside.
- Avoid hazardous apparitions that appear onboard.
- Maintain your sanity. You are completely alone out here.

HERMES will help you—to a point. It actively withholds information it decides you don't need. Piecing together the full picture is up to you, if you even care to. After all, priority number one is just getting through your shift.

![Anomaly Scan](/images/AlwaysHasBeen.png)

## Features

**Dynamic Event System** — Events will happen. The order they occur and which ones you experience are never the same between playthroughs.

**Atmospheric Horror** — The station creaks and groans under the pull of the singularity. Systems fail at random. Reality itself seems to decay around you. Is it madness, or is there something more to it?

**Hidden Story** — There is a reason you are here. There is a reason all of this is happening. Few involved in this deep space endeavor know the truth, and even fewer wish to share it with you.

**Object Physics** — Play with some of the objects scattered around the station. If you want to.

**Short Playtime** — Finish it on a lunch break. A short but concise experience.

**Free** — This game is and will remain free to play.
