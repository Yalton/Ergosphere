# VOID SIGNAL

## Setting
A compact, retro-futuristic research station in stable orbit around a recently discovered black hole. The station consists of just a few key areas: the main observation deck with large reinforced windows facing the void, a cramped control room filled with analog equipment, and a small living quarters/maintenance area. The aesthetics are pure 1970s sci-fi - chunky buttons, flickering CRT monitors, and analog gauges.

## Simplified Gameplay Loop
Four main interactions that can be easily mapped to controller buttons or keyboard keys:
1. **Observe Black Hole** - Point your telescope at specific coordinates and take readings
2. **Adjust Station Position** - Minor orbital corrections to maintain optimal observation angle
3. **Realign Communication Dish** - To maintain contact with Earth as interference grows
4. **Send/Receive Messages** - Log your findings and receive increasingly distorted instructions

## Setup
You are the sole operator of an automated research station, sent to collect data on the black hole for a standard 5-day mission. Your daily routine is simple: wake up, perform the four core tasks, and log your findings. Earth expects regular updates but communications are delayed by several hours.

## Progression
- **Day 1**: Everything functions normally, establishing the routine
- **Day 2**: Minor anomalies begin - instruments show impossible readings, brief power fluctuations
- **Day 3**: The black hole occasionally seems to "respond" to your observations, shifting in ways that defy physics
- **Day 4**: Visual and auditory hallucinations begin; messages from Earth contain contradictions or impossible information
- **Day 5**: The true nature of the entity within the black hole begins to manifest

## The Reveal
What appeared to be a black hole is actually a thin membrane containing an ancient entity that exists outside our understanding of physics. Your "research" activities haven't been observing it - they've been slowly weakening the membrane.

Each telescope observation has been focusing the entity's attention on our reality. Each orbital adjustment has been aligning the station with weak points in the membrane. Each dish realignment has been creating a conduit. Each message has been a form of communication with the entity, not with Earth.

In the final sequence, you discover that the "black hole" is actually beginning to open like an eye, with the station positioned directly in front of its pupil. Earth never sent you there to research - they sent you as an unwitting sacrifice to complete a ritual started by previous missions.

The final choice is simple but impactful:
- Complete the observation sequence, fully awakening the entity (which begins to reach through the membrane toward the station)
- Attempt to destroy the station to seal the membrane (triggering a self-destruct sequence while the entity tries to stop you)

## Development Simplicity
- Limited environment (3-4 small areas)
- Few interactive elements (4 main controls)
- Minimal character models (just the player's hands/tools)
- Focus on atmospheric lighting and sound design rather than complex mechanics
- Short gameplay duration (under 1 hour)

This concept delivers the cosmic horror and eldritch entity reveal you want while keeping the mechanics streamlined and intuitive. The gradual progression allows players to connect with the routine before it's corrupted, making the horror more impactful.
